Field Manual

The complete reference for BREACH — every tab, section, fold and slider in the level editor, plus the systems behind racing, vehicles, signals, multiplayer and sharing. For guided walkthroughs with one-click example projects, open Help & tutorials on the game's home menu instead.

BasicsGetting started

BREACH is a single-file browser FPS with a built-in level editor. Nothing installs; the whole game is one page.

The home menu

  • Deploy — play the current level (your autosaved work, a loaded file, or the default arena).
  • Play campaign / Load campaign — play a sequence of chained levels with carry-over loadout.
  • Multiplayer — host or join a room (see Multiplayer).
  • Build / edit level — open the editor on the current level.
  • Instructions — a quick controls card. Help & tutorials — guided tutorials with loadable example projects. Field manual — this page. Credits — engine libraries plus the asset attributions collected for the current level.
The build number is shown at the bottom-left of the home menu. If a fix doesn't seem to be live, hard-refresh (Ctrl+Shift+R) and check that number first.

Open the editor with Build / edit level on the home menu, or press P during a solo game. The panel on the right is organized into eight tabs; the last tab you used is remembered.

Most sections start collapsed — click a header to expand it. The ? button on the editor toolbar opens this manual. Everything you do in the editor is undoable with Ctrl+Z, and the level autosaves in your browser while you edit (toggle on the Save tab).

Editor referenceBuild tab

Add shape

+ Box, + Sphere, + Cylinder, + Cone drop a primitive in front of the camera. Primitives get the full Material editor and exact box/sphere colliders; searched models use their real mesh shape. A Pillar and the track pieces are also primitives under the hood.

Track builder

Snap-together race track pieces — covered in depth in Track builder & racing.

Search free models

One search box finds free 3D models on Poly Pizza (no key needed) and Sketchfab (needs a free API token — enter it once, everywhere else shows “Token saved · change”). Click a result to place it. Every searched asset is recorded for the level's Credits screen automatically.

Object & selection

With something selected, this section shows: swap/replace model, and the eight folds that give a prop behaviour — animation, locks, signals, dialogue, vehicle, joints, mechanisms, physics & destruction.

Material

For shape primitives: colour, texture map with U/V tiling and rotation, roughness, metalness, and emissive glow. Imported models keep their own materials — the section says so instead of hiding.

Transform & Handles

Transform is the numeric version of the gizmo: exact position, rotation and scale fields. Handles pick the gizmo mode (move / rotate / scale — also 1/2/3) and proportional scaling (4).

Groups

Select multiple props and press Ctrl+G to group them — the group moves, rotates, scales and duplicates as one. Ctrl+Shift+G ungroups.

Editor referenceObject folds — giving props behaviour

Select any prop on the Build tab and open Object & selection. Eight folds, in panel order:

FoldWhat it does
AnimationPlay a model's built-in animation clips — pick the clip, then when it plays (Auto on load, E Activate, or Signals only) and whether it loops or plays once.
LockRequire a key to E-activate the prop: None or the red / blue / gold / green key, plus Consume key on unlock. Keys come from key pickup pads (see Pickups).
SignalsTag the prop and wire cause → effect between props — full reference in Signals, locks & mechanisms.
Dialogue (NPC)Make the prop talkable with E — one line per row, shown as HUD dialogue. Great for hints and flavour.
Vehicle (drivable)Turn the prop into a drivable car — full reference in Vehicle tuning.
Joint (physics)Hinge the object so it swings under physics — doors, levers, see-saws.
MechanismMove / rotate / scale the object over time — platforms, doors, elevators — with an optional multi-point Waypoint path. See Signals, locks & mechanisms.
Physics & destructionMake it dynamic (pushed, thrown, stacked), grabbable with G, breakable with health and debris, or explosive.

Editor referenceWorld tab

Sections: Environment · Auto-generate · Zones

Environment

Movement

Global player physics: Walk speed (4–60) · Run speed (4–100) · Crouch speed (1–40) · Jump (0–30) · Launch power (0.25–4, jump-pad multiplier) · Gravity (5–80) · Eye height (0.4–20).

Lighting

Sun (directional, 0–4) · Sky ambient fill (0–3) · Brightness lift (0–2) · Sun colour · Sky light colour.

Camera & view

Field of view (50–110) · View distance (60–2000) · Fog density & colour · ground grid toggle. Depth of field: focus distance, in-focus range, blur strength. Post-processing: bloom (+ threshold), motion blur, vignette, film grain, saturation, contrast — all compose together, and DoF still works with effects on.

Sky

Browse Poly Haven HDRIs in-editor or paste an HDRI URL; presets Park / Mountain / Room / None; reflection strength (0–3) and sky rotation (0–360°).

Floor & terrain

Floor colour / roughness / metalness, texture with tile W/H and rotation, and a texture search. Terrain: Height (0–18) and Detail (1–8) with Generate / Regenerate / Flatten, plus a sculpt brush (Raise / Lower / Smooth, brush size, strength) painted directly onto the ground.

Walls & arena

Show boundary walls (off = invisible but still solid), wall material/texture controls, and Arena size (15–2000) — half-width of the playable square. Racing levels usually want 300+.

Audio

A looping music track plus per-event sound rows: grenade explosion, coin pickup, powerup pickup, jump, hit marker, enemy kill (with per-enemy-type overrides), player hurt, and the car set — engine loop, brake, skid, boost. A built-in Freesound browser searches free sounds; each pick is credited automatically.

Auto-generate

Procedurally builds a starting layout (cover, buildings, scatter) you then edit by hand. There's also an AI scene builder — describe a scene in a sentence and it searches and places matching models (works best with a Poly Pizza or Sketchfab key).

Zones

Paintable volumes: Audio zones (music/ambience changes inside), Death zones (instant kill — lava, pits), Jump pads (launch with Launch power), Ladders (climbable faces), and Fire zones (damage over time).

Editor referencePlayer tab

Sections: Handles · Object & selection · Transform · Characters

  • Player avatar — import any model as the third-person body; scroll in the preview to zoom.
  • Player start — drag the blue marker to set where the run begins; Facing° (−180…180) sets the initial look direction.
  • Characters — the roster of player & enemy character models, with animation-slot mapping (clips are grouped by taxonomy: locomotion, combat, hits, deaths…). Characters picked here appear in multiplayer's CHARACTER menu too.

Editor referenceEnemies tab

Sections: Enemies · Object & selection · Transform · Enemy gunfire

  • Type picker — choose an enemy type, import a model for it (⤢ Auto-fit size matches the collider), live preview.
  • Collider radius / height — size the damage hit-cylinder only (0 = auto-fit). They no longer affect movement or spacing; toggle Show colliders to see the pink cylinders on live enemies. Offsets re-center off-pivot models.
  • Spawn markers — place exact spawn points with patrol/hunt mode, patrol radius, detect radius, facing, a patrol route, and “Appears on wave N” (0 = every wave). No markers = classic random waves.
  • Enemy gunfire — how enemy fire looks: bolt tip, streak, trail and muzzle flash.

Editor referenceGameplay tab

Sections: Objectives & rules · Pickups · Loot · Inventory items · Build menu · Cutscenes

Objectives & rules is the heart of the level — the full reference is in Objectives & rules. The other sections:

  • Pickups — pads that grant ammo, health, weapons, power-ups, keys and items. See Pickups & inventory.
  • Loot — what enemies and loot boxes drop when destroyed; place loot boxes with a custom model, per-box contents, or random loot.
  • Inventory items — define carryable items: name, type (3D object you can rotate/zoom in the inspector, or a Journal / note text page), description, model, scale, and what using the item does (use-type / key / amount / consume). Hand them out via pickup pads or signals-gated props.
  • Build menu — the in-game radial Deploy menu (Tab): choose which shapes/models players can spawn while playing.
  • Cutscenes — the camera-sequence editor. See Cutscenes.

  • Fonts — separate UI and display fonts from an 18-font set (Chakra Petch, Orbitron, Rajdhani, Press Start 2P, VT323, Russo One, Bungee, JetBrains Mono…).

Editor referenceSave tab

Sections: Level file · Campaign

  • 💾 Save / Clear save — browser save slot; Autosave while editing keeps it fresh.
  • 🔗 Copy share link — the whole level compressed into a URL. See Saving & sharing.
  • ⬇ Export / ⬆ Import .json — the level as a file you can back up, edit or send.

SystemsTrack builder & racing

Laying track

On the Build tab, the Track builder places snap-together road pieces. Press T for top-down view (scroll to zoom), then click pieces — each snaps onto the exit of the selected piece (select any piece to branch from there), or the last one placed. Del removes the selected piece.

PieceGeometry
Start line16 m straight with the checkered band — the lap counter and grid. Every course needs exactly one.
Straight24 m straight
Short8 m straight — the fine-adjustment piece
45° L / 45° REighth-turn, 18 m radius
90° L / 90° RQuarter-turn, 18 m radius
Bank L / Bank RQuarter-turn with camber that eases in and out — flat at both ends, so it joins flat pieces seamlessly
Ramp ↑ / Ramp ↓24 m straight rising/falling 6 m — chain them for hills and jumps

The road is 12 m wide. All lengths are multiples of 8, so closed circuits line up exactly — and when the two ends nearly meet, Close loop bridges the remaining gap with one exactly-stretched straight.

Barrier walls

Per-piece (Barrier walls checkbox on the selected piece) or course-wide (Walls: whole track / Walls: remove all). Walls are a concrete base plus a clear catch-screen to car-body height; they follow banking and ramps.

The Race objective

Gameplay tab → Objective → Race. Settings: Laps to win (1–99), AI rivals (0–6; 0 = time trial), Rival pace (0.4–1.3 — 1.0 races flat-out). Rivals need a closed loop and a placed drivable vehicle to clone. On deploy you're auto-seated on the grid; throttle unlocks at the end of the 3-2-1-GO countdown.

  • Race HUD — position (P n/N), lap counter, live lap time and session best, top-center.
  • Checkpoints — every piece is a checkpoint; cutting the infield voids the lap with a toast.
  • Ghost lap — your best lap replays as a translucent ghost; toggle with G while driving. It's stored per track shape, so editing the course retires the old ghost.
  • Contact — cars collide: rear-end a rival to shunt them, side-swipe to knock them off line. They recover, and so can you.
  • Results — per-lap times with the best starred; if a rival finishes first you'll see RACE LOST.
  • Multiplayer racing — host a co-op room on a race level: standings sync live and the first to finish wins on every screen.

SystemsSignals, locks & mechanisms

These three folds are BREACH's wiring: Mechanism makes a prop move, Signals connect cause to effect, and Lock gates it all behind keys. Together they build doors, elevators, traps, switch puzzles and win conditions.

Mechanism (the “xa” system)

Enable Animated mechanism on a prop, then set what it does — Move X/Y/Z (m), Rotate X/Y/Z (°), Scale X/Y/Z, a Pivot offset, and Duration (s):

  • Playback: ⟳ Loop · ⇄ Ping-pong · → Once.
  • Trigger: Auto (runs on level start) · E Activate (player presses E) · Signals only (waits for a signal).
  • Waypoint path — add waypoints and the prop travels through them (overrides the Move offset): patrol platforms, cable cars, roaming hazards.

Signals

On the Signals fold, give props a tag, then add rules — when something happens to this prop, do something to a tagged target:

When…Meaning
On destroyedThis prop is broken (needs Physics & destruction health)
On EThe player activates it with E
On object placedA tagged object is set down touching it (contact plates, altars) — optionally only from a specific tag
Do…Effect on the target tag
Toggle / Open / CloseDrive the target's mechanism
Play animPlay a named animation clip on the target
UnlockRemove the target's lock
Win levelMission complete (no target needed)
Play cutsceneRun a named cutscene (no target needed)
Set objectiveChange the objective banner text mid-run
Set checkpointDrop a respawn checkpoint here
Play soundPlay a sound URL

A rule can also require an inventory item — “On E, if the player carries the fuse, do Open”.

Locks & keys

The Lock fold requires a key (red / blue / gold / green) before E works on the prop, with optional Consume key on unlock. Keys are granted by key pickup pads (one-shot — they don't respawn), appear as HUD chips, and can carry a custom display name set on the pickup.

Pickup pads

Gameplay tab → Pickups. Toggle spawning, the base disc, and the bounce/rotate animation; then place pads with + Place ahead or edit each spot's kind, transform and (for items) which inventory item it grants. The kinds:

Inventory

Items defined under Inventory items land in the player's inventory (I): 3D objects open in a rotate/zoom inspector, journals open as text pages. An item's use action can heal, grant ammo, or act as a consumable key for signal rules. In campaigns, inventory carries between levels.

Loot & coins

Loot sets what enemies and loot boxes drop. Coins (spawned on kills) are the in-run currency for the shop and weapon attachments.

Rules that shape the run

  • Objective text — the goal banner (players re-read it with J; signals can rewrite it mid-run).
  • Respawn at checkpoint (solo) — death returns you to the last checkpoint instead of ending the run.
  • Wave mode — Random, or Pre-built from your spawn markers (optionally single-pass, no respawns); between-wave upgrade cards in solo.
  • Fall damage — on/off for player and AI, with safe speed and damage scaling. Ragdoll on death. Crushing props — heavy movers deal damage, with min speed.
  • Hands / unarmed — start with fists only, optionally allow picking up weapons found in the level; the flashlight (brightness, cone, softness, range, colour).
  • Hit numbers — floating damage numbers with font size and hit/kill colours.
  • Multiplayer spawn area — confine MP spawns to a circle or drawn polygon.
  • End-screen messages — custom victory and defeat text.

SystemsCutscenes

Gameplay tab → Cutscenes. A cutscene is a list of camera shots; each shot flies through its waypoints with cinematic controls. Cutscene #1 is the intro — toggle Play intro flythrough on deploy — and any cutscene can be fired mid-run by a signal's Play cutscene action.

  • Waypoints — fly the editor camera somewhere and click + Add camera waypoint (current view); per-point Go to / Set / 👁 look-target / clear / delete.
  • Lens — start/end focal length (14–120 mm) for slow zooms.
  • Rack focus — focus start/end distance, blur start/end, focus band: real depth-of-field pulls.
  • Timing — duration (1–120 s), hold at start/end.
  • Dutch — roll the camera up to ±60° across the shot.
  • Framing — Aim at player start or Look along path.
  • Motion — Smooth (ease in+out) / Constant speed / Ease in / Ease out, on a Linear or Smooth (curved) path.
  • Audio — a per-cutscene sound URL, searchable on Freesound.
  • Preview — ▶ the whole cutscene or just the current shot, with a live camera preview window while you edit.

SystemsMultiplayer

Peer-to-peer over WebRTC — no accounts, no servers to rent. One player hosts and shares the 5-character room code (or joiners pick from the Open games list); everyone else joins, and the host's level transfers automatically — including custom models and sounds.

Modes

ModeRules
CO-OPWaves and objectives vs the AI, together — including multiplayer racing on race levels
1 v 1 DUELFirst to N kills
FREE-FOR-ALLEveryone vs everyone (3+)
TEAM DEATHMATCHRed vs Blue, no friendly fire
KING OF THE HILLHold the point — first to N points

PvP hosts can add AI bots (0–7) at Recruit / Regular / Veteran / Elite difficulty, or Fill with bots from the lobby. Players pick a name, character and colour; the lobby shows ready-up state and the host starts the match.

What syncs: player movement and state, enemies, kill credit, coins and pickups, prop animations and mechanisms, unlocks and keys, objective progress, race standings, chat, and the host's custom HUD/sounds.

SystemsSaving & sharing

  • Browser save — one save slot plus autosave while editing.
  • .json export/import — the level (or a whole campaign) as a file. Everything serializes: props, track, vehicle tuning, rules, cutscenes, HUD, and the asset-credit list.
  • Share link — the entire level compressed into a URL. Anyone who opens it plays it instantly. Models re-download from Poly Pizza / Sketchfab on the recipient's machine (Sketchfab models need the recipient's own free token).
  • Challenge links — from the end screen, share the level plus your score to beat.
  • Campaigns — chain levels with carry-over loadout and inventory; share as one file.
  • Credits — every searched model, texture, HDRI and sound is attributed automatically and travels with the level; the Credits screen shows the per-level list.

The community library

Community levels on the home menu is a gallery of player-built levels hosted alongside the game — browse, then Play one or Open in editor to see how it's built (and remix it; loading is undoable).

To publish your own: editor → Save tab → Submit to community library. That grabs a screenshot of your current view as the gallery thumbnail, copies your level JSON to the clipboard, and opens a short submission form on GitHub (a free GitHub account is needed) — paste the JSON, submit, and once the maintainer approves it a bot publishes it to everyone's gallery automatically, credited to you. Levels that use Sketchfab models get a needs Sketchfab token badge, since those download with each player's own free token.

ExtrasTips & troubleshooting

  • Sketchfab search says it needs a token — it's a free API key: sketchfab.com → Settings → Password & API. Paste it once in any search box; every other box switches to “Token saved · change”.
  • A shared level is missing models — Sketchfab downloads require each player's own token; Poly Pizza models need none. Prefer Poly Pizza for levels you plan to share widely.
  • The game looks out of date — hard-refresh (Ctrl+Shift+R) and check the build number bottom-left of the home menu.
  • Performance dips — post-FX sheds passes automatically under load, but the biggest wins are yours: fewer point lights, smaller textures, fewer high-poly Sketchfab models. The adaptive resolution scaler kicks in on sustained drops.
  • My race rivals won't spawn — rivals need a closed track loop (use Close loop) and a placed drivable vehicle to clone. The Save tab's issues panel calls this out.
  • A mechanism won't fire — check its Trigger: Signals only waits for a signal; E Activate may be gated by a Lock. The signal's target tag must match exactly.
  • I can't find a setting — sections remember their collapsed state; expand the section headers. Selection folds (Vehicle, Signals…) only appear with a prop selected on the Build tab.
